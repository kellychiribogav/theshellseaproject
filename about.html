About Us

Hello, everyone! My name is Kelly Chiriboga, I am Ecuadorian-American, and have a leadership role at The Shellsea Project. Our organization operates from a small ranch on the island of Santa Cruz, near the Tortuga Bay area. Here, we provide aid to the Galápagos National Park with monitoring and looking after turtles that have been rescued from illegal trafficking.

Our team is made up of four veterinarians, three technicians, two caretakers, and a group of international volunteers who support the work of our foundation.

Our mission is to determine the state of health of the giant Galápagos tortoises and green sea turtles rescued from trafficking, take care of them until they are ready to return to their natural environment, and ultimately monitor their movements through chips. We also offer lectures on species preservation at schools and encourage people to report any illegal activity involving turtles.

Workdays are typically long. In the early morning, we start by collecting blood samples and oral/cloacal swabs from a subset of turtles to assess their health. We currently work with 185 turtles (endemic to Santa Cruz Island) that were rescued by the Ecuadorian migration authorities. We have placed microchips under their skin so we can identify and monitor them once they are returned to their habitat natural.
