Welcome!

Galápagos Islands’ giant tortoises and green sea turtles face many threats such as deforestation, habitat destruction, pollution, climate change, introduced species, diseases and, above all, illegal trafficking.

The Shellsea Project focuses on performing sanitary evaluations to identify, treat, and contain any diseases our turtles might carry; preserving their health; recovering species that have been victims of illegal trafficking; and raising awareness among the authorities, institutions, and the local population so that they take appropriate measures. It is hard work, but hard does not mean impossible!

In recent decades, several instances of illegal trafficking involving Galápagos’ tortoises and turtles have been documented and exposed by local and international media. Many of these species are threatened with extinction due to exploitation, leaving a current population size of only 10-15% of their historical numbers. A total of fifteen Galápagos giant tortoises are endemic to the archipelago, six of them being critically endangered and three currently extinct. Our foundation, which has been supporting the Galápagos National Park for more than three years, currently works closely with authorities to devise strategic approaches to increase security and monitoring efforts around turtle nesting sites to prevent further trafficking attempts.
